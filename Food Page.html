Malaysian Food

1. Nasi Lemak

Nasi lemak is a traditional dish that is originated from Malay cuisine. It is the most common and popular dish that will found in Malaysia and is refer as the national dish. Its rice cooked with coconut milk and pandan leaf and typically served with boiled eggs, cucumbers, peanuts, and anchovies. The addition of sambal, a hot and spicy chili paste will increase the flavour of it.


2. Nasi Kerabu

Nasi Kerabu is a kind of nasi ulam, which is a distinctive Malay rice dish made with blue rice. It's one of the most favourite breakfast meal among Malaysians, particularly those from Kelantanese communities. It is consumed with different combinations of hard-boiled salted egg, pickles, fish or fried chicken, crackers, and Ulam, a typical local salad. When rice is cooked, the blue color of the rice comes from petals that are utilized as a natural food coloring. Many people also cook the rice with just plain white rice. It is frequently consumed with stuffed peppers and fried keropok.


3. Nasi Kunyit

Nasi Kunyit is a celebration rice dish that is eaten at weddings and other special events in Malaysia. It is made of glutinous rice cooked with turmeric and coconut milk to make the rice turns yellow. Yellow is a signicant of riches prosperity and impressiveness. During the rice-cooking and steaming process, turmeric and coconut milk were added, and occasionally pandan and lemongrass as well. These additions give the yellow rice its attractive colour, pleasant scent, soft texture, and flavorful taste. Additions of spices like bay leaves, cinnamon, cardamom, and cloves can improve the flavor of this fragrant rice meal.


4. Rendang

Rendang is a Minangkabau dish. It is a meat-based dish that is slow-cooked, braised in coconut milk, and seasoned for hours with a blend of herbs and spices such as turmeric, ginger, chilli, lemongrass, garlic, shallot, and kaffir lime leaves. As the liquids evaporate, the meat gets soft and dark brown, caramelizes, and absorbs rich flavors.


5. Roti Jala

Roti jala is a common teatime snack enjoyed by Malaysians. It is a classic Malay meal, generally cooked and served at festivals and weddings. It is typically consumed as an alternative to rice in sets of three or four pieces with curries, particularly chicken curry. The principal components are flour, eggs, coconut or dairy milk, and a small amount of turmeric. They are mixed with water to create a thin batter, which is then poured in a heated pan.


6. Curry

Curry is believed to have originated in India and spread to many parts of the world, including Malaysia. It is made out of a sauce or gravy seasoned with a blend of ground spices. Onion, ginger, and garlic are used to provide the base of many curries. Curry powders are high in turmeric, coconut milk, shallots, ginger, belacan, and chili peppers are frequently used in Malaysian curries.


7. Char Kway Teow

Char Kway Teow is one of the most epic noodle dishes in the world from the streets of Malaysia. In Hokkien and Teochew, char means 'stir-fried' and kway teow refers to flat rice noodles. It is made from flat rice noodles or kway teow of approximately 1 cm or about 0.5 cm in width, stir-fried over very high heat with garlic, light and dark soy sauce, chili paste, whole prawns, shelled blood cockles, chopped Chinese chives, slices of Chinese sausage, and bean sprouts.


8. Satay

Satay is a traditional Malay delicacy - marinated beef, mutton, and chicken are skewered and grilled over moderate heat, then dipped in a thick layer of sauce made from peanut butter, coconut sauce, baby shrimp, etc. Satay and sauce enter the mouth together. It is extremely fragrant and will make you nostalgic after eating it. Some locals also like to enjoy skewers of satay with traditional Malay rice, cucumbers, and onions wrapped in coconut leaves or pandan leaves to make the satay taste more distinct in the mouth.


9. Rojak

Rojak is a light snack like a vegetable and fruit salad. Add the unique local shrimp paste sauce and lemon juice, and it is served with pineapple, cucumber, water, mango, and fried ghost shrimp cakes. It is served with a big mix them all together in a ceramic "pot" and sprinkled with ingredients such as crushed peanuts or sesame seeds on top. Common rojak include fruit rojak or Indian rojak, which have different flavors and characteristics.


10. Asan Laksa

Asam Laksa is a slightly sour soup made from fish (mackerel). "Asam" locally is a tropical sour tamarind fruit, and it is also the main source of the sour taste of Asam Laksa. Ingredients include red pepper, lemongrass, galangal flowers, turmeric, shallots, and other spices added to the fish soup and cooked together. Is a delicacy usually served with rice noodles or rice noodles, cucumber, pineapple, green onions, lettuce, mint leaves, bean sprouts, etc. Rich soup, very delicious.


11.Nasi Kandar

The name of the Nasi Kandar originated from the early Penang hawkers who would hang rice and various curries on both ends of the pole and sell it at the docks and construction sites. The main ingredients of this dish are pork, chicken, fish, and vegetables. The curry of the Nasi Kandar is made by boiling four kinds of curry, red, green, white, and yellow, in a pot respectively, and pouring coconut milk into it. The coconut milk added can reduce the spiciness of the curry and increase the aroma, making the curry taste very mild. Finally, add the side dishes and fry the fish. This Nasi Kandar dipped in curry makes people feel spicy and a little sweet. It is delicious and full of Nanyang flavor.


12. Roti Canai

Roti canai is a round, flat, or square and crispy pancake. Usually, the filled roti canai are square with thin slices of bread. Mix the dough, divide it into small balls, and then rotate it with a technique similar to "spreading a tablecloth" After several tossing, the cake becomes very large and thin. Fold it from the outside inward into a round cake with a diameter of five to six inches or a square cake of five to six square inches, and then let it fall into the pot. Pan-fry it well, so the skin is crispy, and serve it while hot with Dal sauce, also called vegetarian curry or curry, and anchovy chili sauce. It is rich in flavor and you will never get tired of eating it.


13. Murtabak

Murtabak's recipe is made with spicy ground lamb, eggs, and onions that are encased in sealed flour in an envelope shape. The stuffed crepe or paratha is then pan-fried to give it a crispy outer shell. This appetizer is cut into squares or halves and is usually served with curry or eaten on its own as a main course.


14. Mee Rebus

Mee Rebus is a Malay food. The yellow noodles are topped with a special spicy potato soup, and topped with hard-boiled eggs, fried dried tofu, bean sprouts, chopped green onions and fried shallots, freshly squeezed lime juice, and the soul of this dish - the sauce. Moreover, the thickness of the sauce is the key to the deliciousness of this food. It is one of the specialty noodles in Malaysia.


15. Wonton Mee

There are mainly two types of Wonton Mee in Malaysia: soup noodles and dry noodles. Generally speaking, Malaysians mostly eat dry wonton noodles. "Gan Lao" is the Malaysian term for lo mein. Mix the cooked noodles with the sauce composed of dark soy sauce, sun oil, sweet soy sauce, sesame oil, soy sauce, scallion oil, and oyster sauce, then add barbecued pork, green vegetables, and a bowl of soup wontons. And eat it with a little pickled green pepper.
