Malaysian Beverage

1. Teh Tarik

Teh tarik is a popular hot milk beverage that is most frequently found in mamaks, outdoor stalls and kopitiams in Malaysia. The drink's name comes from the preparation method of continuously pouring the drink back and forth with arms extended, which helps to slightly cool the tea and create its own unique texture. It is made from a strong brew of black tea blended with condensed milk. It's also Malaysia National beverage.


2. Air Barley

Air Barley is a clear, thick beverage that tastes well with additional sugar and lime. This beverage is created by boiling entire barley pearls until the liquid becomes somewhat thickened due to the starch leaking out. Lime juice is added to lighten it and sugar to sweeten it. The whole barley pearls are soft, thick, and sugar-sweetened, were stirred into the drink. It is commonly accessible in coffee shops, street food vendors, and hawker stands.


3. Air Tebu

Air tebu, or sugarcane juice, is a popular and pleasant beverage. Peeled sugar cane is crushed in a mill to produce air tebu. This sweet, green juice is a popular drink for both residents and visitors. It is frequently served during religious ceremonies and gatherings, including festivals and weddings. In many homes, it is also a sign of welcoming for the visitors.


4. White Coffee

White coffee is a popular drink originated in Ipoh, Malaysia. It is served with condensed milk after the coffee beans are roasted using palm oil margarine. The part of the brewing process has no additional material or ingredient add to the coffee beans. It is just roasted with margarine, not sugar, giving a coffee a lighter colour which makes it unique because other forms of coffee are often roasted with sugars, margarine, and wheat.


5. Air Cincau

Air Cincau is a thin liquid cold drink that is a traditional specialty drink in southwestern Fujian and is simply brewed from the gelatinous food made by boiling grass jelly. Often comes with gelatinous grass jelly cut into strips. Add the appropriate amount of rock sugar to increase the antipyretic feeling of grass jelly water. Sometimes it is mixed with soy milk or milk and eaten. It is one of the essential drinks to relieve the heat in summer.


6. Air Sirap

Air sirap is a popular Malaysian drink that can be translated to "syrup water" in English. It is a sweet and refreshing drink made from a combination of rose syrup, water, and sometimes a squeeze of lime juice. The rose syrup used in air sirap is usually made from a mixture of sugar, water, and rose water, which gives the drink its distinct floral aroma and flavor.


7. Air Mata Kucing

Malaysia's Frozen Longan Special Drink Air Mata Kucing translates as "cat's eyes" in Malay, which is the stewed winter melon with mangosteen, longan, and rock sugar that is famous in Chinatown, Malaysia. It tastes sweet, clears away heat, and relieves heat. It brings a refreshing sensation in the hot summer and is deeply loved by adults and children.


8. Air Limau Asam Boi

Air Limau Asam Boi translates to lemon sour plum water. It is a must-have summer drink made of lemon slices and sour plums. In addition to clearing away heat and quenching thirst, its sour and sweet taste can also help people whet their appetites and improve their taste experience. Some people also add more sugar to neutralize the sourness and only retain the flavor and aroma of lemon and sour plum.
